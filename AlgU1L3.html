Rational and Irrational Numbers

Can a number be both rational and irrational? No, but real numbers fall into one or the other of these two categories. Indeed, the set of real numbers contains both rational and irrational numbers

The set of real numbers includes many subsets of numbers. This is an integral part to the study of algebra and will help you understand many other concepts in this course.

Bases and Exponents

In the expression 34 the 3 is referred to as the base and the little 4 is the exponent. 34 is read as “three to the fourth power.” It means to multiply 3 (the base) by itself 4 times. The base is the number that is multiplied. The exponent tells how many times to multiply the base number by itself.

Square Roots

A square root is one of two equal factors of a given number. The radical sign is used to represent the square root.

The square root of a number can either be rational or irrational. A number is irrational if its decimal is either NOT repeating or NOT terminating. Otherwise, it is a rational number.
